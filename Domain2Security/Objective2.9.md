# 2.9 Compare and contrast common data destruction and disposal methods

# Physical Destruction of Hard Drives

## Drilling

Physically damages the drive platters by drilling holes through the drive.
Example: Using a power drill to destroy an HDD before disposal.

## Shredding

Destroys storage devices by mechanically shredding them into small pieces.
Example: Industrial shredder destroying old SSDs and HDDs.

## Degaussing

Uses strong magnetic fields to erase magnetic storage media permanently.
Example: Degausser wiping data from old hard drives and tapes.

## Incineration

Destroys storage devices completely through high-temperature burning.
Example: Secure government disposal using industrial incineration.

# Recycling or Repurposing Best Practices

## Erasing/Wiping

Securely overwrites existing data to prevent recovery.
Example: Using DBAN to wipe a hard drive before resale.

## Low-Level Formatting

Completely resets the drive structure and sectors at a very low level.
Example: Manufacturer utility performing low-level SSD preparation.

## Standard Formatting

Removes file system references but may not fully erase recoverable data.
Example: Quick formatting a USB drive in Windows.

# Outsourcing Concepts

## Third-Party Vendor

External company hired to handle secure disposal or recycling of devices.
Example: Business using certified e-waste disposal services.

## Certification of Destruction/Recycling

Official documentation proving devices were securely destroyed or recycled.
Example: Vendor providing a certificate after shredding drives.

# Regulatory and Environmental Requirements

Rules governing secure disposal of data and environmentally safe recycling practices.
Example: Company following e-waste disposal regulations and privacy laws.
